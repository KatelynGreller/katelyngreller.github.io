I'm Katelyn Greller.

I’m a philosophy B.A. and UX grad seeking opportunities in NYC. I became interested in HCI when I started thinking about the ethical imperatives inherent to persuasive design. As a UX student I’ve contributed to projects for BOPA and MHEC. I also blog about tech here. I’m obsessed with Post-It notes, pencils and pens.



MHEC Information Architecture

 When the Maryland Higher Education Commission approached our class to redesign their IA, my group saw an opportunity to reposition MHEC as the state’s premiere higher education access resource. I took a leading role in developing personas, scenarios, and brand strategy to reflect the key themes of access, success, and innovation.

BOPA App Design

 The Baltimore Office of Promotion & the Arts was looking to close the gap between public art and public citizens with a mobile app that lets residents suggest murals in their own communities. My group prototyped our proposed app from low to high fidelity with an emphasis heuristic research and microinteractions.


HCI Case Study

For my coursework in HCI I designed a case study based on nudging behavior in a defined user population. From researching challenges in the area of voice UI I discovered that digital assistants are underutilized for online shopping. Using concepts from our work on influence, persuasion and perception, I proposed a solution that uses light and sound to encourage voice purchases.
